# Legal Disclaimer & Responsible Use Policy

## Scope of Use

This toolkit is designed exclusively for:

- **Authorized security research** on systems owned by or explicitly permitted by the researcher
- **Defensive security operations** within an organization's own infrastructure
- **Academic and educational research** in controlled environments
- **Threat intelligence** using publicly available, passive data sources only

## Prohibited Use

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

- Unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data
- Active scanning or probing of systems without explicit written authorization
- Use of this toolkit to facilitate any illegal activity under applicable law, including but not limited to the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA, US), the Computer Misuse Act (CMA, UK), and equivalent legislation in other jurisdictions
- Use against critical infrastructure, government systems, or healthcare systems without authorization
- Any use that violates GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, or other applicable data protection regulation

## Responsible Disclosure

All vulnerability research conducted using this toolkit follows responsible disclosure principles:

1. Vulnerabilities are reported privately to the affected vendor or organization before public disclosure
2. A reasonable remediation period is provided (typically 90 days unless a zero-day exploit is actively being used)
3. CVE assignment is sought through appropriate channels (MITRE, vendor CNA)
4. Disclosure is coordinated with the affected party

For disclosure timelines and published CVEs, see [`research-notes/disclosures/`](research-notes/disclosures/).

## Data Privacy

This toolkit does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data. All analysis is performed locally. Where research involves personal data subject to GDPR, UK GDPR, or CCPA, appropriate data minimization and purpose limitation principles apply.
